Source: EIA installed net summer capacity (all fuels, all sectors). Load-growth forecast: Grid Strategies (2025).

Methodology & data

Both lines are installed generating capacity (net summer capability, all fuels, all sectors), indexed to 2015 = 100. Texas grew capacity 43.7% (roughly 51 GW) over the decade — outpacing its ~29% demand growth — while U.S. capacity grew just 15.6%. We use installed capacity, not net generation, as the supply measure: net generation tracks demand almost by identity and so can't show whether supply kept pace, while capacity is the stock of productive capital. The scale of the challenge ahead: the U.S. added about 166 GW of capacity over this entire decade, yet Grid Strategies now projects roughly that same 166 GW of load growth in just five years — so meeting it will require building much faster, closer to Texas's pace than the country's recent average. Sources: EIA State Electricity Profiles (existing net summer capability), via the EIA Open Data API (2015–2024); Grid Strategies, Power Demand Forecasts Revised Up for Third Year Running (2025).
